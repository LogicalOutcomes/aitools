## Program overview

Pathways to Work is a 12-week employment readiness program serving newcomers to Canada in Hamilton, Ontario. The program addresses a critical gap: professionally-trained newcomers who face significant barriers to securing employment in their fields due to credential recognition challenges, lack of Canadian work experience, and limited familiarity with Canadian workplace culture and job search practices.

The program serves adults aged 18 and older who arrived in Canada within the past five years and hold valid work authorization. Most participants bring professional backgrounds from their home countries but struggle to translate these credentials and experiences into Canadian employment opportunities. Through a structured three-phase model, participants move from skills assessment and career planning, through intensive job search preparation, to hands-on work placements with local employers.

Pathways to Work operates as a cohort-based program, running four cohorts annually with approximately 20 participants each. The program combines individual career counselling, group workshops on Canadian workplace culture, practical skill development in resume writing and interviewing, mentorship matching with local professionals, and supervised work placements with 40+ employer partners. Follow-up support continues for six months after program completion to track employment outcomes and provide additional assistance as needed.

### Target population

| Characteristic | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| **Age range** | Adults 18 years and older |
| **Eligibility** | Arrived in Canada within past 5 years; hold valid work authorization; typically have professional backgrounds from home countries |
| **Geography** | Hamilton, Ontario and surrounding area |
| **Delivery** | In-person at Hamilton Newcomers Employment Centre; work placements at employer partner sites |

### Key activities

1. **Skills inventory and assessment** — Comprehensive evaluation of professional credentials, work experience, and transferable skills completed during weeks 1-4
2. **Career planning and goal setting** — Individual counselling sessions to identify realistic employment targets aligned with Canadian labour market opportunities
3. **Canadian workplace culture workshops** — Group sessions addressing workplace norms, communication styles, and professional expectations specific to Canadian employment contexts
4. **Resume and cover letter development** — Individualized support to translate international credentials and experience into Canadian-format application materials
5. **Interview preparation and practice** — Mock interviews with employer volunteers providing feedback on presentation, responses, and cultural navigation
6. **LinkedIn profile optimization and networking training** — Digital presence development and strategies for building professional networks in Hamilton
7. **Professional mentorship matching** — Pairing with working professionals in participant's field for guidance, industry insights, and connection-building
8. **Supervised work placement** — Two days per week for four weeks with employer partners, providing Canadian work experience and potential hiring pathways
9. **Bi-weekly individual career counselling** — Ongoing one-on-one support throughout the 12-week program to address barriers and adjust strategies
10. **Post-placement follow-up** — Check-ins at 3 months and 6 months after program completion to track employment outcomes and provide additional support

## Evaluation objectives

This evaluation will increase the program's capacity to:

1. **Meet participant needs** and help participants achieve their employment goals in their professional fields.
2. **Achieve program goals and outcomes** related to securing meaningful employment for newcomers, particularly placement in roles aligned with their professional backgrounds and credentials.
3. **Improve program quality and fidelity** to the three-phase model, ensuring consistent delivery of assessment, skill-building, and placement components across all four annual cohorts.
4. **Increase responsiveness to participants** in how services are delivered, particularly regarding the relevance of workshops, quality of work placements, and effectiveness of mentorship matching.
5. **Increase accessibility and equity** in service provision for diverse newcomer populations with varying language levels, credential types, and professional backgrounds.
6. **Increase responsiveness to key interest groups** including employment counsellors, employer partners hosting placements, volunteer mentors, funders (Ontario Ministry of Labour, Immigration, Training and Skills Development and United Way Halton & Hamilton), and the broader Hamilton business community.

## Evaluation questions

This evaluation will address the following questions, customized for Pathways to Work:

1. **What activities are provided by the program?** Specifically: skills inventories, career planning sessions, resume development support, workplace culture workshops, mock interviews, mentorship matching, LinkedIn training, work placements, and follow-up contacts.

2. **To what extent is the program being implemented as designed and meeting quality standards?** Specifically: adherence to the 12-week three-phase model, completion rates for each phase, quality and relevance of work placements to participants' professional backgrounds, consistency of bi-weekly counselling, and effectiveness of mentorship matching.

3. **What are the characteristics of the populations served by the program?** Specifically: countries of origin, professional backgrounds and credential types, length of time in Canada, language proficiency levels, age ranges, and employment goals.

4. **What do participants think about services and what are their suggestions?** Specifically: usefulness of workshops on Canadian workplace culture, quality and relevance of work placements, helpfulness of mentor relationships, effectiveness of resume and interview preparation, and barriers encountered during the program.

5. **What do staff and other key interest groups think about services and what are their suggestions?** Specifically: employer partner feedback on participant readiness and placement quality, mentor experiences and observations, employment counsellor insights on participant barriers, and funder perspectives on program outcomes.

6. **To what extent are participants meeting their goals?** Specifically: completion of skills inventory and career plan, development of Canadian-format resumes, completion of work placements, establishment of professional networks, and achievement of employment goals.

7. **What short-term and mid-term changes are participants experiencing?** Specifically: increased confidence in navigating the Canadian job market, improved job search skills, employment within 3 months of program completion, employment in professional field or related role, and sustained employment at 6 months.

8. **What evidence suggests the program is contributing to its desired long-term impact?** Specifically: patterns in sustained employment beyond 6 months across multiple cohorts, evidence of career advancement among program alumni, and trends in successful credential recognition or professional pathway development over time.

## Evaluation framework

This framework includes 11 measures across three categories: outputs (including fidelity), short-term outcomes (including participant experience), and mid-term outcomes.

**Each monthly staff meeting should result in at least one documented service improvement or issue for investigation** — collect feedback through interviews and tracker data, review monthly in staff meetings, discuss improvements with Program Manager, implement service changes, document changes in tracker and meeting notes, and repeat.

### Outputs

| Outputs/Outcomes | Indicators | Data Source/Method | Frequency |
|------------------|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Number of participants served | Count of participants enrolled per cohort | Participant tracker — intake records | Quarterly |
| Program completion | Percentage completing all three phases | Participant tracker — phase completion field | Quarterly |
| Work placement completion | Percentage completing 2 days/week placement | Participant tracker — placement attendance records | Quarterly |
| Timely program access | Median days from intake to program start | Participant tracker — intake and cohort start dates | Monthly |
| Participant-informed practice | Number of documented service improvements | Staff meeting notes | Monthly |

### Short-term outcomes

| Outputs/Outcomes | Indicators | Data Source/Method | Frequency |
|------------------|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Skills inventory and career plan completion | Percentage completing by week 4 | Participant tracker — phase 1 completion field | Monthly |
| Resume and LinkedIn profile development | Percentage with updated materials by week 8 | Participant tracker — phase 2 completion field | Monthly |
| Job search confidence | Participant-reported confidence levels | Small-sample interviews (5-10 participants per cohort) | Monthly (themes reviewed) |

### Mid-term outcomes

| Outputs/Outcomes | Indicators | Data Source/Method | Frequency |
|------------------|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Employment at 3 months | Percentage employed post-program | Participant tracker — 3-month follow-up field | Annually |
| Field-related employment | Percentage employed in professional field | Participant tracker — employment match field | Annually |
| Sustained employment | Percentage still employed at 6 months | Participant tracker — 6-month follow-up field | Annually |

### Long-term outcomes (Aspirational — not directly measured within evaluation scope)

> **Note:** Long-term outcomes are aspirational goals that will likely not be measured directly within the scope of this evaluation. Progress toward these outcomes is inferred from patterns in mid-term data collected over multiple years.

| Outputs/Outcomes | Indicators | Data Source/Method | Frequency |
|------------------|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Career advancement | Employment retention patterns | Aggregated mid-term data | Annually (Year 2+) |
| Economic integration | Movement from survival to professional roles | Longitudinal analysis | Annually (Year 2+) |
| Community workforce contribution | Newcomer professionals in Hamilton labour market | Employer partner feedback over multiple years | Annually (Year 2+) |

## Project roles

An evaluation project is a PROJECT, meaning it requires clear ownership. Without someone accountable for leadership and someone managing day-to-day coordination, evaluation projects stall.

### Assigned roles

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| **Sponsor** | Executive Director or Senior Manager champions evaluation at leadership level; ensures staff time and resources are allocated; approves annual evaluation reports; removes barriers to data collection; implements strategic recommendations from evaluation findings |
| **Liaison** | Program Manager serves as primary contact; manages internal scheduling of review meetings; coordinates between evaluation activities and program delivery; translates evaluation findings into operational language for staff |
| **Project Owner** | Program Manager is accountable for evaluation approach and quality; ensures evaluation framework is followed; validates findings; conducts small-sample interviews with participants; prepares annual comprehensive reports; makes decisions about evaluation methodology |
| **Project Manager** | One Employment Counsellor (rotating annually) handles day-to-day coordination; maintains participant tracker data quality; monitors monthly review schedule; prepares feedback summaries for staff meetings; generates quarterly reports from SharePoint; ensures tracker fields are completed consistently |

> **For this program:** The Program Manager (0.5 FTE) can serve as both Liaison and Project Owner given the strategic nature of the role. However, operational coordination (Project Manager role) should rotate annually among the two Employment Counsellors to build evaluation capacity across the team and distribute workload.

### Program staff involvement

Program staff are not assigned a specific evaluation role but participate in:

- Monthly feedback review meetings
- Implementing service improvements based on evaluation findings
- Contributing observations about participant barriers and program effectiveness to the participant tracker

## Schedule

### Evaluation phases

**Phase 1: Planning and initiation (Weeks 1-4)**

- Program Manager and Employment Counsellors review this evaluation plan and customize for organizational context
- Engage funders and employer partners to confirm evaluation priorities
- Enhance SharePoint List participant tracker with recommended fields: participant goals in own words, phase completed dropdown, placement sector/role match field, employment at 3 months field, employment at 6 months field, service adjustments notes
- Establish baseline data for current cohort participants
- Assign Project Manager role to one Employment Counsellor

**Phase 2: Design and development (Weeks 3-8)**

- Develop interview protocol for small-sample interviews with 5-10 participants per cohort at mid-program (week 6-7) and 3 months post-completion
- Create standard SharePoint reports for monthly and quarterly reviews
- Train all staff on continuous improvement cycle and monthly review process
- Conduct first round of interviews with current cohort
- Hold first monthly staff meeting using new review format

**Phase 3: Implementation (Ongoing)**

- Employment Counsellors collect participant feedback during bi-weekly counselling sessions and record themes in tracker notes field
- Project Manager conducts small-sample interviews at week 6-7 of each cohort and at 3 months post-program
- Monthly staff meetings review short-term outcome data and fidelity indicators from tracker
- Employer Relations Coordinator conducts quarterly check-ins with 8-10 employer partners and shares themes in staff meetings
- Staff implement at least one documented service improvement or issue for investigation per month based on feedback
- Quarterly progress reports generated from participant tracker

**Phase 4: Analysis and reporting (Quarterly + Annual)**

- Quarterly: Project Manager generates output reports and short-term outcome summaries; 2-page dashboard shared with Program Manager, Executive Director, and funder liaisons
- Annual: Program Manager prepares comprehensive evaluation report including full-year outputs, mid-term outcomes (employment at 3 and 6 months), participant stories from interviews, service improvements implemented, trends across cohorts, and recommendations
- Annual report presented to funders (Ontario Ministry and United Way), employer partners, community partners, and organizational board
- Multi-year trends analyzed after 2+ years to identify patterns suggesting progress toward long-term outcomes

### Reporting schedule

| Report | Frequency | Audience | Content |
|--------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Feedback highlights | Monthly | Employment Counsellors, Employer Relations Coordinator, Program Manager in staff meetings | Themes from participant interviews and counselling sessions; employer partner observations; process flags like completion delays; at least one documented service improvement or issue for investigation |
| Progress report | Quarterly | Program Manager, Executive Director, funder liaisons (Ontario Ministry of Labour, Immigration, Training and Skills Development; United Way Halton & Hamilton) | Output indicators (enrollment, completion rates); short-term outcome trends; summary of service improvements made; upcoming cohort plans; 2-page dashboard format |
| Comprehensive evaluation | Annual | Funders, employer partners, volunteer mentors, community partners, organizational board, senior management | Full-year outputs; mid-term outcomes (employment rates at 3 and 6 months); participant stories and quotes from interviews; service improvements implemented throughout year; trends across all four cohorts; comparison to previous years; recommendations for next year; 8-10 page report with data visualizations |

### Evidence-informed approach

This plan's indicators and data-collection choices were informed by a focused scan of research on employment programs for skilled newcomers to Canada. The evidence is *suggestive* of program components associated with stronger outcomes — formal credential assessment, employer connection, and combined language-plus-work supports — and was used to inform, not dictate, the indicators above.
