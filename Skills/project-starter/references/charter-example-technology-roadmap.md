## 2. Project Summary

### 2.1 Background & Context

This project responds to NSS's need to analyze its technology platforms and create a plan for technology investment for its online programs over the next five years.

NSS currently supports several web-based programs (including a main public website, a youth portal, and a staff intranet) using different technology platforms, including Drupal and an outdated version of Microsoft .NET.

The multiplicity of platforms may be an unnecessary burden on NSS in terms of staffing, project management, and costs. NSS hopes to reduce costs by consolidating and reducing technology expenditures. Specifically, NSS has staff dedicated to online services spread across three projects. In the future, staff should work together across all online services, minimizing the use of external contractors.

### 2.2 Overall Purpose

This project aims to assess NSS's technology options, focusing on web content management in the context of all its technology platforms throughout the agency, given its budget constraints and program plans over the next few years.

### 2.3 Key Outcomes and Benefits

The main outcome will be a decision by NSS senior management and technology staff regarding NSS's technology platforms for its online services, with clear directions for developing a detailed implementation plan.

## 5. Assumptions

<details>
<summary><strong>Standard Assumptions</strong> (click to expand)</summary>

1. **Timely feedback**: The client organization will respond to requests for feedback and relevant information within the scheduled time set in the project plan. Unless agreed otherwise, responses to questions and feedback on draft deliverables will be provided within three working days.

2. **Working group oversight**: A working group will be formed to provide strategic direction and oversight to the project and provide timely approval of deliverables and requests to support or changes to the project plan.

3. **Background documents**: The client organization will provide background documents and, if appropriate, lists of stakeholders for interviewing, within four weeks of project launch unless otherwise stated in the project schedule.

4. **Scope management**: Minor changes to the workplan and deliverables can be made with joint approval of client and LogicalOutcomes throughout the project. Substantive changes or increases must be negotiated, either by replacing elements of the original workplan or by increasing the budget.

5. **Acceptance timeline**: Unless the Project Sponsor requests changes, the final deliverables will be accepted as approved four weeks after their submission.

</details>

## 6. Risk Assessment

### Major Risks — High Likelihood or High Impact

| Risk | Mitigation | L | I |
|------|------------|:-:|:-:|
| **Tech team not engaged** in analysis process | Working group reviews processes; shared documents for ongoing feedback; clear decision criteria; share emerging findings before finalizing | M | H |
| **Managers lack right information** for platform decision | Engage Sponsor to define decision criteria at start; review emerging findings throughout project | M | H |
| **Tasks take longer** than anticipated | Use agile methods to deliver initial recommendations every 2-3 weeks; decide which need further work | M | H |
| **Staff don't provide input** within timelines | Assign working group to provide feedback; escalate to Sponsor if needed | M | H |
| **Managers don't implement** the recommended roadmap | Define implementation commitment at project start; present roadmap options with clear trade-offs | H | H |
| **Confidentiality breach** | Password-protect interview notes; store confidential material in separate labelled directories | M | H |
