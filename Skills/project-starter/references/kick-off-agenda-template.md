# Communications Assessment

For projects with a training component, ensure the training team is identified and contact information captured in the project notebook.

If the team requires access to Microsoft products, request a Microsoft account for each team member (see Onboarding tab).

## Conduct the Communications Assessment

After the project kick-off, meet with team members one-on-one to determine:

- Preferred method of communication
- Language preferences
- Availability
- Concerns or suggestions regarding communication and learning style

Also confirm they have the technical support needed — screen share and check they have access to the tools required for the project.

# Internal Project Kick-off

Use the project notebook to structure the meeting.

1. Introduce everyone and their roles on the project
2. Briefly remind everyone how we use our project tools and ask for questions; follow up later for more detailed questions or for new team members
3. Review project charter and active tasks
4. Discuss the project and get a feel for the activities, challenges, issues, opportunities — this should be facilitated but freely flowing, to give us all a chance to be creative and find out what's happening
5. What are the benefits of the project (i.e. outcomes)? Where is the opportunity to create tools and develop skills? Take time for the team to connect with the project: share hopes and fears
6. Debrief
7. Assign tasks (using active tasks in the notebook)
